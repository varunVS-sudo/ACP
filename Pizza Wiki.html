**Pizza** is an Italian dish of leavened wheat dough topped with tomato , cheese, and other ingredients, baked at high temperature.

The term *pizza* was first recorded in 997 AD in Gaeta. Raffaele Esposito created modern pizza in Naples.
Neapolitan pizza became an EU [TSG] dish in 2009 and joined UNESCO intangible cultural heritage list in 2017.

Pizza is among the world's most popular foods, sold at pizzerias Mediterranean restaurants, via delivery, and as street food.

In Italy, restaurant pizza is served unsliced and eaten with utensils; casually, it's cut and eaten by hand. Grocery stores sell frozen pizza and preparation kits.

In 2017, the global pizza market was $128 billion; the US market was $44 billion. 13% of Americans eat pizza daily.
Pizza is the world's second most eaten food after rice.
